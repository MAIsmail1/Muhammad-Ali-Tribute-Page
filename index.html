Muhammad Ali

One of the most significant and celebrated sports figures of the 20th century

"Being a true Muslim is the most important thing in the world to me. It means more to me than being black or being American."

-- Muhammad Ali

Here's a time line of Muhammad Ali's life:

  • 1942- Born in Louisville, Kentucky, USA
  • 1954- Began training as an amateur boxer aged 12
  • 1960- Won gold in light heavyweight division at the Summer Olympics and turned professional later that year
  • 1961- Reverted to Islam
  • 1964- Won the world heavyweight championship from Sonny Liston in a major upset aged 22
  • 1965- Ali defended his title against former heavyweight champion Floyd Patterson
  • 1966- Refused to be drafted into military duty due to religious beliefs and ethical opposition to the Vietnam War. He was found guilty and stripped of his boxing titles
  • 1970- Stayed out of prison while appealing the decision to Supreme Court, where his convition was overturned
  • 1971- Ali's and Fraziers first fight, nicknamed the "Fight of the Century". Ali lost by unanimous decision, his first professional defeat
  • 1972- Ali fought Jerry Quarry, had a second bout with Floyd Patterson and faced Bob Foster in 1972, winning a total of six fights that year
  • 1973- Ken Norton broke Ali's jaw while giving him the second loss of his career. However, he won his second bout which led to a rematch with Frazier
  • 1974- Judges awarded Ali a unanimous victory in January, which set up a fight against George Foreman
  • 1974- In October, Ali caused a major upset, after Ali came in as a 4-1 underdog against undefeated Foreman. "Rumble in the Jungle" was watched by a record audience of 1 billion viewers
  • 1975- Third match with Frazier, titled "Thrilla in Manila". Frazier's eyes were both swollen shut. Ali, in his corner, winner by TKO, slumped on his stool, clearly spent
  • 1981- Officially retired from Boxing after a series of small bouts
  • 1984- Diagnosed with Parkinson's Disease due to head trauma from boxing
  • 2012- Awarded the Philadelphia Liberty Medal in recognition of his lifelong efforts in activism, philanthropy and humanitarianism
  • 2013- Ali could no longer speak and was told he could be dead in a matter of days
  • 2016- Ali was hospitalised with respiratory illness. His condition worsened and died from sepsis shock aged 74
